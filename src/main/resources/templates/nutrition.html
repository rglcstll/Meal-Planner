Understanding Nutrition

Macronutrients: The Building Blocks

Macronutrients are the nutrients your body needs in larger amounts: carbohydrates, proteins, and fats. Each plays a vital role in maintaining health and providing energy.

  • Carbohydrates: Your body's main source of energy. Found in grains, fruits, vegetables, and legumes. Choose complex carbs like whole grains for sustained energy.
  • Proteins: Essential for building and repairing tissues, making enzymes and hormones. Found in meat, poultry, fish, dairy, beans, lentils, and tofu.
  • Fats: Important for energy storage, cell function, and absorption of fat-soluble vitamins. Focus on healthy unsaturated fats from sources like avocados, nuts, seeds, and olive oil.

Micronutrients: Vitamins & Minerals

Micronutrients are vitamins and minerals needed in smaller amounts but are crucial for various bodily functions, including energy production, immune function, and blood clotting.

A balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats will typically provide an adequate supply of most micronutrients.

Calorie Balance

Understanding calorie balance is key to managing weight. Calories are units of energy your body gets from food and drinks.

  • Calorie Deficit: Consuming fewer calories than your body burns, leading to weight loss.
  • Calorie Maintenance: Consuming the same number of calories your body burns, maintaining your current weight.
  • Calorie Surplus: Consuming more calories than your body burns, leading to weight gain (can be muscle or fat).
